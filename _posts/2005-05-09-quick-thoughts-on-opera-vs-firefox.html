I've just about achieved a 50/50 balance in Opera/Firefox usage. Right now I'm kinda cheesed with Opera, as a software company. The 7.5 browser is awesome but its starting to annoy me that every major point zero release (eg 6.0,7.0,8.0) they've offered has had (what I believe should be) show-stopper bugs.That's part, but not all of the reason I started up Firefox. As I mentioned, I was keen to try the del.icio.us plugin which turns out to be pretty cool. And now that I'm using Firefox, I'm stoked to see the effective search integration with dictionary.com (as I'm not a great spellar) and other search engines. Opera has the mult-search thing going on but its not quite as nice, or extensible.If Opera had opened things up a bit with a flexible plugin framework I think they'd be doing a lot better. They have a pretty sound product that should be increasing its market share significantly as the average user wakes up and runs from IE. Tim Bray's regularly updated Browser Market Share shows a consistent ~3% for Opera while Firefox makes steady gains, currently around 45% (Mozilla family). I put that down to the hype generated by the developer community who are motivated to extend upon the core browser functionality, not freeness or an Open-Source license.It's just so refreshing some competition is returning to the browser industry. I just hope Opera see why firefox is doing so well and embrace contributions from the user/dev community.
