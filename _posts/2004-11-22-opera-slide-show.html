This morning I went to check out Joe Gregorio's XML Review slides which he mentioned were published in the new and cool-sounding (name and purpose) S5.

First impression was... what the hell is Opera doing with this page??? It rendered a single page with Zero formatting. So I took a look at the S5 overview and it says its compliant with Opera Show... what is that? A quick google and it turns out that Opera Show is enabled simply by going full screen - F11. This now renders the slides properly and you can control the slide show with keys PgUp/PgDn keys. Try it on the Opera Show Tutorial, it looks pretty powerful. I've been using Opera for 3 years and I didn't know this feature existed. Call me stupid. But it would be really nice if Opera alerted me to the fact that the page I'm browsing is actually an Opera Show and I should check it out by going full screen.

I wonder how many slide show's I've missed because I didn't know they were show's and Opera didn't let me know? Probably not many because its a format that only Opera supports... but with S5 being nice and 'open', there should be more.
