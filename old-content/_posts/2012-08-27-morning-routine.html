I'm pretty sure I've read Brad Feld's explanation of his morning routine at least a couple times but for some reason I never got around to implementing my own version.

A little over a week ago I made a plan to spend time each morning on four areas that I believe are really important to making a great start to the day: physical health, mental agility, relationships, planning. Here they are:

First I'll wake up and go drink some water which takes about five minutes. This is just the simple act of killing the alarm, stopping the Fitbit, visiting the bathroom and grabbing 1-2 glasses of water. Aside from the calls of nature, if there's some job or task I feel I need to take care of, I'll only let myself go so far as to put it in the to-do list in my phone.

I spend the next ten minutes working through a set of stretches. I enjoy stretching and find it a good way to give my mind some time to warm up. I'm repeating each stretch twice and holding each for 25 seconds. This seems to be a good trade of between time and the depth of the stretch (before running I'll do 3 x 30 sec.).

Since I'm now pretty well energised and alert, I launch into some basic excercises including a combination of situps, pushups, planks. I alternate and mix this up everyday to keep it interesting. This takes about ten minutes and gets the blood flowing good and proper.

The next activity is somewhat experimental. I'm practicing a breathing routine that takes less than five minutes but I it think will help me better manage the nerves I get right before speaking in front of an audience. I'll see how this goes and maybe switch it out at some point.

Following the breathing, I'm nice and calm so it's a good time to reflect and focus on what's going on around me for five minutes or so. During this time I tick off in my mind successes/achievements of previous day(s), then consider the areas and means for making improvements. I keep this fairly high-level, vision/goal oriented and avoid worrying about the day-to-day.

All the above happens in the living room but I'll now shift to my office desk and log-in to my laptop spend the next fifteen minutes rounding up my to-do list / calendars / email. I follow the Getting Things Done methodology so this is really a quick review where I check what I've got on during the day ahead and see what I want to focus on.

To wrap up I'll spend around ten minutes on News & General Reading. I have a pretty set list (my fav. Aussie news website, Hacker News, plus a couple blogs). I skim through these fairly quickly and just pick of a couple interesting stories to read. Thanks to Twitter and the social web, somebody is going to tell me about anything truly important that I might miss.

One last thing, the wildcard is breakfast which I take whenever my girlfriend wakes up and wanders into the room. I'm still adjusting and need to focus on this, but the idea is to embrace the interruption and spend fifteen minutes enjoying the company.

All up, this is one hour and fifteen minutes and so far feels about right. Of course I also need to get dressed, shower and shave, and iron a shirt, but that's do-able.
