Engadget have pics and details of Sony's new portable audio devices. Featuring flash memory, the models announced are available in 256Mb, 512Mb and 1Gb varieties.The good news for me is that a couple of the models include FM radio functionality, something that I've been chasing in the past. I checked out Sony's range in January while shopping in Tokyo but I wasn't really taken by any of their products and any case I had no money left after making my other purchases.The Sony release is also covered at c-Net. Towards the bottom of the c-net article, an image of a Sony-Ericsson phone links off to a story from earlier in the week about new phones that use the iconic Walkman branding. These new phones feature 512Mb capacity for storing high quality audio, as well as a 2 MegaPixel camera. Forgive the tangent, I'm about to make a point. I really like the convergence of portable audio, mobile telecommunication and digital photography.In fact love it. I wonder if Sony are concerned about market canibalisation? Afterall, they have a big stake in each area. No matter, I'll let them figure it out while I take a photo of myself listening to music and talking to my mates at the same time. Stay tuned for that!
